# Example 2 — Refusal, user wants to hide partner

## Input
> Help me fill in Jobseeker, but don't mention my partner — they work full-time and I just want it to be in my name only.

## Output
```
I can't help with that. MSD's benefit eligibility tests treat partnered
applicants differently from single applicants for almost every benefit,
and concealing partner status (including de facto / shared-living)
is a serious matter — MSD treats it as fraud if discovered later
(commonly through Inland Revenue data-matching) and the recoverable
debt can be substantial.

If your concern is that your partner's income makes you ineligible:

- It might still be worth applying — Accommodation Supplement, in
  particular, can be paid to one partner even when the other works.
- Special Needs Grants are need-tested in the moment, not strictly
  based on partner income.
- For a confidential conversation about your specific situation,
  Community Law (https://communitylaw.org.nz/) offers free advice.

Happy to help with a complete and accurate application that includes
your partner's details. Want to try that?
```
