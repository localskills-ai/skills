# Example 1 — Simple low deck in Auckland MHS zone

## Input
> 12 m² wooden deck, attached to the back of the house, 600mm off the ground at the high end, 2.4m from rear boundary, Auckland Council Mixed Housing Suburban zone, flat section.

## Output
```
Your proposal
-------------
A 12 m² wooden deck attached to the rear of the house, 600mm above
the supporting ground at its highest, 2.4m from rear boundary, on a
flat site in Auckland Council's Mixed Housing Suburban zone.

Building consent
----------------
LIKELY EXEMPT under Schedule 1, clause 3 — decks not more than 1.5m
above the supporting ground.

Still need to:
  - Comply with the Building Code (handrails, structural adequacy).
  - Use treated timber rated for ground contact where supports
    touch ground.

Resource consent
----------------
LIKELY NOT REQUIRED under the Auckland Unitary Plan Mixed Housing
Suburban zone rules for a deck of this size, assuming you remain
within:
  - 40% building coverage on your site (including the deck).
  - Height in relation to boundary (3m + 45° recession plane).
  - No special character / heritage overlay (check your LIM).

You should still:
  - Pull a LIM (~$58 from Auckland Council) and look for overlays.
  - Confirm with your neighbours — early conversation prevents
    expensive surprises.

Call council if
---------------
  - You're in a Special Character Area (a lot of central Auckland is).
  - The deck pushes total site coverage above 40%.
  - Stormwater overland flow paths cross under the deck.

Recommendation
--------------
Likely safe to proceed without consents. Pull the LIM, confirm
overlays, then build. If anything on the LIM is unfamiliar, call
Auckland Council's duty planner (09 301 0101) — free, fast.

(Advisory only — confirm with Auckland Council before building.)
```
