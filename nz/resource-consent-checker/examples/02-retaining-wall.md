# Example 2 — Retaining wall on the boundary in Wellington

## Input
> 1.8m high retaining wall along the rear boundary, holding back a sloping garden bed. Wellington City Council zone Inner Suburban. 8m long.

## Output
```
Your proposal
-------------
A 1.8m high × 8m long retaining wall along the rear boundary,
retaining a sloping garden bed, Wellington City Council Inner
Suburban zone.

Building consent
----------------
LIKELY REQUIRED.

Schedule 1, clause 20 exempts retaining walls that retain **not more
than 1.5m depth of ground** with no surcharge from buildings or
driveways. At 1.8m you're above the exemption — building consent
required.

Resource consent
----------------
DEPENDS — check with Wellington City Council.

Wellington's district plan has rules on earthworks volume / area and
boundary works in some zones. For a wall this size, a resource
consent isn't automatic but it's not safe to assume "no" either.

Call council if
---------------
  - Earthworks behind the wall exceed your zone's permitted
    volume / area thresholds.
  - The wall affects an overland flow path or stream margin.
  - The neighbour's site is materially affected.

Recommendation
--------------
Apply for a building consent — non-negotiable at 1.8m. Before
lodging, ring Wellington City Council's duty planner
(04 499 4444) to ask if a resource consent is also needed for
your specific site. The pre-application chat is free and saves
the cost of a missed condition later.

A chartered structural engineer's design will likely be required
for the building consent at this height.

(Advisory only — confirm with Wellington City Council before building.)
```
